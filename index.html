Why Net Worth Matters

Members' equity — the club's net worth — is the single most important long-term health indicator. It represents the cumulative value that members collectively own: total assets minus total liabilities. A rising trend signals that the club is building value, not consuming it.

GHCC's net worth has grown from $4.82M in 2019 to $8.67M in May 2026 — a 80% increase ($3.85M) over seven years. This growth reflects three reinforcing factors:

1. Debt Reduction — The Tri Counties loan has declined from $6.8M (2019) to $5.3M, reducing annual interest by ~$90K and freeing cash for reinvestment.

2. Capital Stock Growth — Capital stock rose from $5.4M to $8.5M through initiation fees and membership transfers, funded without additional debt.

3. Asset Reinvestment — Capital projects ($4.9M over 5 years) maintained and improved facilities while depreciation naturally reduced fixed asset book values.

◆ CMAA Benchmark: Clubs that grow net worth at 5–8% annually are considered financially healthy. GHCC's 7-year CAGR of 8.7% places it in the top quartile of comparable private clubs nationally.
